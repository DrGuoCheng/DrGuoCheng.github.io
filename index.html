About me

I am Guo Cheng, an incoming Tenure-Track Assistant Professor of Computer Science at Texas A&M University–Corpus Christi (TAMU-CC), starting in August 2026. My research is in computer vision, autonomous driving, and deep learning, with a particular focus on perception and scene understanding for safety-critical systems.

I received my Ph.D. in Computer Science from Purdue University in 2022, advised by Professor Jiangyu Zheng, with a dissertation titled "Sequential Semantic Segmentation of Streaming Scenes for Autonomous Driving." Before joining TAMU-CC, I spent 2022–2025 as a Software Engineer at Bloomberg L.P. in New York, where I worked on post-trade processing, regulatory reporting systems, and developed/maintained large language models (LLMs) for Bloomberg law-document understanding — integrating closed-source APIs and fine-tuning open-source models on PyTorch and AWS.

My work has been published in IEEE T-ITS, IEEE T-IV, ICPR, IV, and ITSC. I earned an M.S. in Statistics from Stony Brook University and a B.S. in Information and Computer Science from Wuhan Textile University.

At TAMU-CC, I am building a research group that bridges deep-learning research with the engineering rigor required to deploy AI in the real world. I am actively recruiting Ph.D. students. Please see the prospective students page.

Selected projects

Sequential Semantic Segmentation for Streaming Driving Scenes

Ph.D. dissertation work on memory-efficient, sequential semantic segmentation of high-resolution driving video, with applications to path and speed planning for autonomous vehicles. Published in IEEE T-ITS and ICPR.

PyTorch · TensorFlow · OpenCV · CNN/Transformer

Vision-Language Models under Perceptual Degradation

Recent preprint studying when and how VLMs lose semantic alignment under noise, blur, weather, and other perceptual perturbations — with implications for safety-critical perception pipelines.

VLMs · multimodal · robustness

Big-Video Mining of Driving Appearances

Mining large-scale driving footage to model weather and illumination, supporting all-weather road-edge detection and robust perception. Published in T-IV and ITSC.

video mining · sensor fusion · ADAS

LLMs for Bloomberg Law-Document Understanding

At Bloomberg, developed and maintained large language models for law-document understanding — integrating closed-source APIs and fine-tuning open-source models with PyTorch on AWS.

LLMs · PyTorch · AWS · RAG

Trade Processing & Regulatory Reporting (Bloomberg)

Enhanced latency-control for post-trade processing via routing functions with new attributes (C++), and developed dynamic data models that automated the analytics lifecycle from raw data to actionable trading intelligence (Python).

C++ · Python · production systems

ClearVision for Pipes (ClearObject)

Designed encoder–decoder deep-learning models and built the codebase for bounding-box object detection and data visualization, with object detection and tracking for damage of wooden boards in surveillance footage.

TensorFlow v1/v2 · Computer Vision
